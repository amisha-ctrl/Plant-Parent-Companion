Watering:

Most foliage plants prefer to be kept evenly moist but not soggy. Water when the top 1-2 inches of soil feels dry. Reduce watering during the dormant period (usually winter or cooler months) to avoid overwatering.

Light:

Most foliage plants thrive in bright, indirect light. Avoid direct sunlight as it can scorch the leaves. Low-light foliage plants are also available for darker indoor spaces.

Temperature:

Most foliage plants prefer temperatures between 65¬∞F to 75¬∞F (18¬∞C to 24¬∞C). Avoid sudden temperature changes and drafts.

Humidity:

Many foliage plants enjoy higher humidity levels, especially if they are tropical or subtropical varieties. Regular misting or placing them near a humidifier can help maintain humidity.

Soil:

Use a well-draining potting mix to prevent root rot. Avoid heavy, compacted soils that retain too much moisture.

Fertilizer:

Feed with a balanced houseplant fertilizer once a month during the growing season (spring and summer). Reduce feeding in fall and winter.

Pruning and Maintenance:

Remove yellowing or dead leaves to encourage healthy growth. Regularly wipe the leaves with a damp cloth to keep them clean and free from dust.
